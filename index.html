What is nearshore software development?

Nearshore software development is the practice of building software teams in nearby countries with strong time zone overlap. For US companies, Latin America is often used for nearshore engineering because teams can collaborate during the same workday.

Why do CTOs hire software developers in Latin America?

CTOs hire software developers in Latin America to improve time zone overlap, expand engineering capacity, reduce hiring friction, and access strong technical talent across countries such as Mexico, Colombia, Brazil, Argentina, Chile, Peru, Costa Rica, Uruguay, Ecuador, and Guatemala.

What are Agentic AI Dev Workflow teams?

Agentic AI Dev Workflow teams are software teams designed to work with AI coding tools, automation, review systems, and modern delivery workflows. The goal is not just faster code. The goal is safer planning, better review, stronger ownership, and cleaner production delivery.

What are the risks of traditional nearshore staff augmentation?

Common risks include inflated rates, weak vetting, resume first matching, high churn, underpaid engineers, unclear ownership, disconnected EOR providers, device delays, access risk, and poor delivery governance.

Why does AI change nearshore team design?

AI tools help teams move faster, but they can also make mistakes faster. CTOs and CIOs need people who know when to use AI, when to check the work, and when to stop bad code before it reaches production.
